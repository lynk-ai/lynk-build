name: Host preparations (USA)
description: How the United States prepared its 11 host venues and secured its 78 matches for the 2026 World Cup — stadium retrofits and the federal security operation. Read when you need the logistics and security story behind the US leg of the tournament, rather than the tournament's format or results.
---

All 11 US host venues were existing NFL stadiums — among them MetLife Stadium, AT&T Stadium, SoFi Stadium, Mercedes-Benz Stadium, Gillette Stadium, NRG Stadium, Hard Rock Stadium, Arrowhead Stadium, and Lumen Field. Several installed temporary natural-grass pitches over their usual artificial turf to meet FIFA's playing-surface requirements — sourced (CBS Sports / meetstadium.com). The full 16-city split across the US, Mexico, and Canada, and the 78-of-104 US match share, is covered in [format-and-overview](format-and-overview.md).

Security for the US leg was a federally backed operation. FEMA awarded $846 million in grants to the 11 US host cities, $625 million of it through the dedicated FIFA World Cup Grant Program, covering security, cybersecurity, and drone-threat protection — sourced (FEMA.gov, corroborated by HSToday/CISA reporting). Each of the 78 US matches was designated a National Special Security Event (NSSE), the classification the US government uses for events warranting the highest tier of federal security coordination — sourced, same sources.

On the ground, more than 400 law enforcement agencies coordinated with federal agencies and private security firms across stadiums, fan festivals, team base camps, and hotels — sourced (ESPN).

The tournament's opening match was played outside the US, at Estadio Azteca in Mexico City — see [format-and-overview](format-and-overview.md) for that result. Mexico's own run through the tournament, including how that opening result fit into an unbeaten group stage and a Round of 16 exit, is covered in [upsets-and-storylines](upsets-and-storylines.md).
