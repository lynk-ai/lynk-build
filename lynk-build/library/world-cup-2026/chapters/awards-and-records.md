name: Awards and records
description: The 2026 World Cup's individual awards (Golden Ball, Golden Boot, Golden Glove, Best Young Player, Fair Play) and its tournament-wide records for attendance and goals scored. Read when you need who won a specific award, or a tournament statistic, rather than a match result.
---

**Individual awards.** Rodri (Spain) won the Golden Ball as the tournament's best player; Lionel Messi finished second in the voting, and Kylian Mbappé won the Bronze Ball — sourced (Goal.com).

Kylian Mbappé (France) won the Golden Boot with 10 goals, his second consecutive World Cup Golden Boot. Messi won the Silver Boot with 8 goals and 4 assists — sourced (NBC Sports/Goal.com).

Unai Simón (Spain) won the Golden Glove, recording 7 clean sheets in 8 matches, conceding only 1 goal across the whole tournament, and putting together a 650-minute shutout streak. That clean-sheet count is a new record: the previous mark was 5 clean sheets, shared by seven different goalkeepers across past tournaments — Jongbloed (1974), Zenga (1990), Taffarel (1994), Barthez (1998), Kahn (2002), Buffon (2006), and Casillas (2010) — sourced (FIFA.com/Fox Sports).

Pau Cubarsí (Spain), a defender born 22 January 2007 (age 19 at the tournament), won Best Young Player. He played every minute of Spain's run as the team conceded just one goal all tournament — sourced (FIFA.com/The Analyst, Opta).

The Netherlands won the FIFA Fair Play Award — the first time the Dutch team has won it, and the 11th different nation to win the award since it began in 1970 — sourced (NL Times/Caliber.Az).

**Attendance.** Total tournament attendance was 6,810,966, an average of 65,490 per match at 99.7% stadium occupancy — nearly double the prior record of roughly 3.59 million, set at USA 1994 — sourced (Al Jazeera, citing FIFA).

**Goals scored.** This was the highest-scoring World Cup in history, surpassing the 172-goal record set at Qatar 2022; the record-breaking 173rd goal was scored by the USA's Auston Trusty against Türkiye. The tournament finished with 308 goals across its 104 matches — about 2.96 goals per game, the best scoring rate since 1970 — sourced (FIFA via Al Jazeera "World Cup 2026 in numbers"; Daily Star/Sofascore).

**Messi's final tournament.** In his sixth and final World Cup, Messi became only the second man after Cafu to play in three separate World Cup finals (2014, 2022, 2026) — sourced (ESPN stats series).
