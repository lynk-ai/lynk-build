Spain won the 2026 FIFA World Cup, its second title, beating Argentina 1–0 after extra time in the final at MetLife Stadium in East Rutherford, New Jersey, on 19 July 2026, in front of 80,663 spectators. Ferran Torres scored the winner in the 106th minute off a header assist from Nico Williams. Argentina played the closing stretch a man down after Enzo Fernández was sent off in the 90th minute, and Argentina goalkeeper Emiliano Martínez made 11 saves, a World Cup final record — sourced (Wikipedia "2026 FIFA World Cup final"; NBC News). The round-by-round path both finalists took to reach this match is in [knockout-routes](knockout-routes.md).

With this win, Spain became the first nation to hold the men's and women's World Cup titles simultaneously, having won the 2023 Women's World Cup — sourced (Wikipedia).

The third-place match, played in Miami on 18 July 2026, went the other way for the two teams that lost their semifinals: England beat France 6–4 — the most goals scored in any World Cup third-place match. England led 4–0 at half-time on goals from Declan Rice, Ezri Konsa, and two from Bukayo Saka (37' and first-half stoppage time). In the second half, Saka completed his hat-trick with an 87th-minute penalty and Jude Bellingham added a stoppage-time goal, for England's six; France scored all four of its goals in the second half, two from Kylian Mbappé and one each from Bradley Barcola and Ousmane Dembélé — sourced (NBC/Olympics.com/ESPN/Euronews/Al Jazeera).

The semifinals that set up both the final and the third-place match: Spain beat France 2–0 in Dallas, and Argentina beat England 2–1 in Atlanta (Anthony Gordon opened the scoring for England in the 55th minute; Enzo Fernández equalized for Argentina in the 85th; Lautaro Martínez won it in the 90th+2nd minute). This was the first World Cup in which all four FIFA top-ranked teams reached the semifinals — sourced (Yahoo/CBS Sports; Wikipedia).
