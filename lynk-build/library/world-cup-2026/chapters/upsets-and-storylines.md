Mexico entered the knockout rounds unbeaten through its first four matches, with three clean sheets, extending an unbeaten run of 10 straight World Cup matches at Estadio Azteca (a run that opened this tournament, per [format-and-overview](format-and-overview.md)). That run ended in the Round of 16, where England beat Mexico 3–2 (Mexico 2–3 England) at Estadio Azteca in Mexico City on 6 July 2026 — a result that closed out Mexico's 40-year bid to reach a quarterfinal. Jude Bellingham scored twice and Harry Kane converted a penalty for England; Raúl Jiménez scored a penalty among Mexico's goals. England played roughly the last 40 minutes of the match with 10 men after Jarell Quansah was sent off — sourced (FIFA.com match centre "Mexico v England 2–3 Round of 16"; ESPN; England Football; Al Jazeera; Olympics.com).

Germany suffered its first-ever World Cup penalty-shootout loss, eliminated in the Round of 32 by Paraguay after a 1–1 draw — a result multiple outlets called the tournament's biggest upset. Paraguay's own path there was unlikely: the team opened the tournament with a 4–1 loss to the United States, then upset Turkey and drew Australia to reach the knockout rounds — sourced (Al Jazeera/Fox Sports).

Canada won its first-ever World Cup knockout match, beating South Africa 1–0 in Los Angeles. Elsewhere in the Round of 32, Morocco beat the Netherlands on penalties after a 1–1 draw, and Brazil beat Japan 2–1 on a last-minute winner from Gabriel Martinelli — sourced (Yahoo Sports).
