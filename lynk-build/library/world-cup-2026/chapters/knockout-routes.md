Spain's route to the title ran through five knockout matches. In the Round of 32, Spain beat Austria 3–0 in Los Angeles (two goals from Mikel Oyarzabal, one from Pedro Porro) — sourced (Olympics.com/FIFA.com). In the Round of 16, Spain beat Portugal 1–0 in Dallas on a Mikel Merino goal in injury time — the match that turned out to be Cristiano Ronaldo's last at a World Cup — sourced (FIFA.com match centre "Portugal v Spain 0–1 Round of 16"; Olympics.com; ESPN). In the quarterfinal, Spain beat Belgium 2–1 (Fabián Ruiz's opener and a Mikel Merino winner in the 88th minute, against a Charles De Ketelaere goal for Belgium — the first goal Spain conceded all tournament) — sourced (FIFA.com match centre; ESPN; CNN; Al Jazeera; Olympics.com). In the semifinal, Spain beat France 2–0 in Dallas (Mikel Oyarzabal, Pedro Porro) — sourced (FIFA.com/Olympics.com). In the final, Spain beat Argentina 1–0 after extra time — the final itself is covered in full in [the-final-and-podium](the-final-and-podium.md).

Argentina's route to the final ran through its own set of five knockout tests. In the Round of 32, Argentina beat Cape Verde 3–2 after extra time in Miami (Messi 29', Lautaro Martínez 92', and a Diney Borges own goal in the 111th minute, against two Cape Verde goals from Duarte (59') and Lopes Cabral (103')) — sourced (FIFA.com match centre "Argentina v Cabo Verde 3–2 Round of 32"; ESPN; Al Jazeera). In the Round of 16, Argentina beat Egypt 3–2, coming back from a two-goal deficit late to win with a stoppage-time goal — Egypt itself having reached this round by beating Australia in the Round of 32 — sourced (FIFA.com match centre "Argentina v Egypt 3–2 Round of 16"; NPR; Olympics.com). In the quarterfinal, Argentina beat Switzerland 3–1 after extra time in Kansas City (Alexis Mac Allister 10' from a Messi cross, Julián Álvarez 112', and Lautaro Martínez in second-half extra time (120+1'), against a 67th-minute goal from Dan Ndoye for Switzerland; Switzerland's Breel Embolo was sent off in the 72nd minute) — sourced (FIFA.com match centre; ESPN; Al Jazeera; NPR). In the semifinal, Argentina beat England 2–1 in Atlanta — details in [the-final-and-podium](the-final-and-podium.md). In the final, Argentina lost to Spain 1–0 after extra time.

The group-stage form that got all four semifinalists into the knockout rounds, per final group standings (sourced, CBS Sports/Wikipedia/ESPN/Fox Sports):

| Team | Group | Record | Points | Results |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Argentina | J | 3-0-0 | 9 | beat Algeria 3–0, beat Austria 2–0, beat Jordan 3–1 |
| France | I | 3-0-0 | 9 | beat Senegal 3–1, beat Iraq 3–0, beat Norway 4–1 |
| Spain | H | 2-1-0 | 7 | drew Cabo Verde 0–0, beat Saudi Arabia 4–0, beat Uruguay 1–0 |
| England | L | 2-1-0 | 7 | beat Croatia 4–2, drew Ghana 0–0, beat Panama 2–0 |
